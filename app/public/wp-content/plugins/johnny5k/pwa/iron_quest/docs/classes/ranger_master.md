# Johnny5k: IronQuest

## Ranger Class - Master Design Document (v1)

## Overview

The **Ranger** is the movement and endurance-focused class in IronQuest.

Rangers thrive on:

- Cardio
- Steps
- Travel efficiency
- Sustained performance

They are designed for users who:

- Enjoy walking, running, or cardio
- Want flexible progression
- Like steady, consistent growth

## Core Identity

> "Keep moving. The path is yours."

## Primary Playstyle

- Earn Travel Points quickly
- Chain cardio and workouts
- Progress across the map faster than other classes

## Core Bonuses

| Bonus Type      | Effect                                          |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Step XP bonus   | +20% XP from steps                              |
| Cardio XP bonus | +15% XP from cardio                             |
| Travel bonus    | +1 bonus Travel Point per qualifying travel day |

## Base Stats

| Stat              | Value                             |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Max HP            | 100                               |
| Recovery scaling  | Balanced                          |
| HP loss tolerance | Slightly better endurance scaling |

## Subclasses

### Scout

Fastest mover in the game, built around exploration and route efficiency.

#### Identity

- Fastest mover in the game
- Exploration focused

#### Strengths

- Extra travel efficiency
- Lower supply costs

#### Weaknesses

- Lower combat burst

### Hunter

Hybrid subclass that converts movement into combat effectiveness.

#### Identity

- Cardio-to-combat conversion
- Balanced field performance

#### Strengths

- Gains combat bonuses from cardio
- Balanced performance

#### Weaknesses

- Requires hybrid playstyle

### Beastmaster

Consistency and daily-engagement subclass with passive support flavor.

#### Identity

- Consistency and daily engagement
- Habit-based power curve

#### Strengths

- Bonuses from streaks and habits
- Passive support effects

#### Weaknesses

- Less burst power

## Gameplay Systems Interaction

### Travel System

Rangers are the strongest class for:

- Map traversal
- Unlocking locations quickly
- Reaching bosses faster

### Heavier Lift Mechanic

Rangers are not the strongest class for heavy-lift mechanics, but they can offset that weakness by:

- Reaching locations faster
- Pairing cardio with missions for stronger daily value
- Taking endurance-friendly mission routes

### Travel Bonuses

- Extra Travel Points from activity
- Bonus XP from movement

### Boss Synergy

Rangers perform best when:

- Cardio is completed before missions
- Travel is completed on the same day

## Boss Mechanics Interaction

Rangers gain advantages when:

- Entering boss fights after a strong travel day
- Pairing cardio with the same-day mission
- Maintaining stamina across longer encounters

### Example Boss Bonuses

- +1 boss progress if cardio is completed the same day
- +2 final boss roll if travel is completed the same day

## Visual Evolution System

### Overview

Rangers evolve visually based on movement mastery.

This evolution is reflected in:

- AI-generated character portraits
- Equipped gear visuals
- Post-boss Victory Portraits

The goal is to create:

> "I can see how far I've traveled, not just how much XP I've earned."

### Evolution Reflection Points

- Greater environmental confidence
- More advanced travel gear
- More commanding movement posture

### Evolution Goal

- Make progression feel fast, wide, and visibly earned

### Evolution Tiers

| Tier   | Level Range | Identity          |
| ------ | ----------- | ----------------- |
| Tier 1 | 1-2         | Explorer          |
| Tier 2 | 3-5         | Tracker           |
| Tier 3 | 6-8         | Hunter            |
| Tier 4 | 9-12        | Elite Ranger      |
| Tier 5 | 13-15       | Mythic Pathfinder |

### Tier 1 - Explorer

#### Identity

- Unproven traveler
- Early route learner

#### Visual Style

- Simple travel gear
- Light starter weapon
- Minimal ornamentation

#### Tone

- Curious
- Hopeful

#### Pose Direction

- Forward-leaning stance
- Alert but not dominant

#### Environment

- Training Grounds
- Open paths and soft daylight

### Tier 2 - Tracker

#### Identity

- Competent route reader
- Early field specialist

#### Visual Style

- Better cloak silhouette
- Trail wear on equipment
- Sharper utility details

#### Tone

- Focused
- Confident

#### Pose Direction

- Active mid-stride stance
- Weapon or travel tool visible

#### Environment

- Early wild routes
- Light weather or terrain effects

### Tier 3 - Hunter

#### Identity

- Battle-ready movement specialist
- Endurance-proven adventurer

#### Visual Style

- More defined armor layering
- Movement-focused silhouette
- Stronger gear detail

#### Tone

- Capable
- Hardened

#### Pose Direction

- Aggressive or pursuit-ready stance
- Dynamic action framing

#### Environment

- Forests, cliffs, long roads
- Clear signs of active pursuit or survival

### Tier 4 - Elite Ranger

#### Identity

- Route master
- Dominant travel specialist

#### Visual Style

- Detailed cloak and armor accents
- High-quality pathfinder gear
- Subtle environmental aura

#### Tone

- Commanding
- Calm under pressure

#### Pose Direction

- Elevated, surveying stance
- Confident travel posture

#### Environment

- Large world-map environments
- Height, wind, weather, or motion effects

### Tier 5 - Mythic Pathfinder

#### Identity

- Avatar of movement
- World-crossing legend

#### Visual Style

- Iconic cloak silhouette
- Mythic travel gear
- Natural or magical motion effects

#### Tone

- Unstoppable
- Larger-than-life

#### Pose Direction

- Heroic and mobile
- Central, cinematic framing

#### Environment

- Epic terrain
- Horizon-scale world visuals
