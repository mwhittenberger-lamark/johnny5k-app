# Johnny5k: IronQuest

## Rogue Class - Master Design Document (v1)

## Overview

The **Rogue** is the accessibility, efficiency, and momentum-focused class in IronQuest.

Rogues thrive on:

- Shorter workouts
- Flexible schedules
- Efficient progression
- Gold generation

They are designed for users who:

- Are new to fitness
- Feel intimidated by longer workouts
- Want quick wins and steady progress
- Value flexibility over intensity

## Core Identity

> "Start small. Stay sharp."

## Primary Playstyle

- Complete shorter workouts consistently
- Stack small wins into meaningful progression
- Earn and utilize gold efficiently
- Maintain momentum even with limited time

## Core Bonuses

| Bonus Type                 | Effect                                           |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Short workout bonus        | +20 XP for short workouts                        |
| Quick-session streak bonus | +10 XP for quick-session streaks                 |
| Gold gain bonus            | +20% gold gain from missions, meals, and rewards |
| Economic advantage         | Faster economic progression than other classes   |

## Base Stats

| Stat              | Value                                                    |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Max HP            | 90                                                       |
| Recovery scaling  | Lower HP demands due to shorter sessions                 |
| HP loss tolerance | More forgiving mechanics to compensate for lower base HP |

## Subclasses

### Assassin

Precision and execution-focused subclass.

#### Identity

- Precision and execution
- Clean, efficient finishing

#### Strengths

- Strong short-session performance
- Better burst in clean encounters

#### Weaknesses

- Lower value from drawn-out fights

### Thief

Economy and resource-control specialist.

#### Identity

- Gold generation
- Economy mastery

#### Strengths

- Strongest economy scaling
- Better access to items, travel, and flexibility

#### Weaknesses

- Lower direct combat ceiling

### Shadow

Flexibility and forgiveness-focused subclass.

#### Identity

- Mobility and adaptation
- Lower punishment for imperfect play

#### Strengths

- Strongest recovery from bad rhythm
- Best for inconsistent schedules

#### Weaknesses

- Less explosive than Assassin

## Gameplay Systems Interaction

### Short Workout System

The Rogue's primary advantage comes through shorter sessions and efficient completion.

### Heavier Lift Mechanic

Rogues are not built around heavy-lift mechanics, and instead convert their advantage through:

- Short-session access
- Lower friction progression
- Faster gold and route flexibility

### Gold System

Rogues dominate the economy via increased gold gain.

### Travel System

Rogues use gold to fast travel more frequently and smooth over schedule gaps.

### Boss Synergy

Rogues perform best with:

- Clean, efficient sessions
- Strong first-half execution
- Smart economy and prep choices before the fight

## Boss Mechanics Interaction

Rogues gain advantages when:

- Entering fights with efficient setup
- Keeping sessions clean and compact
- Using gold and supplies to reduce friction before the encounter

### Example Boss Bonuses

- +2 boss roll for clean runs at higher levels
- Stronger payoff for efficient, low-error sessions

## Visual Evolution System

### Overview

Rogues evolve visually through sharper silhouettes, cleaner gear, and stronger stealth identity.

This evolution is reflected in:

- AI-generated character portraits
- Equipped gear visuals
- Post-boss Victory Portraits

The goal is to create:

> "I look faster, cleaner, and more dangerous with every tier."

### Evolution Reflection Points

- Better stealth gear
- More refined movement silhouette
- Stronger confidence in pose and environment control

### Evolution Goal

- Make efficiency and stealth feel aspirational rather than small-scale

### Evolution Tiers

| Tier   | Level Range | Identity      |
| ------ | ----------- | ------------- |
| Tier 1 | 1-2         | Basic Rogue   |
| Tier 2 | 3-5         | Sneak         |
| Tier 3 | 6-8         | Assassin      |
| Tier 4 | 9-12        | Phantom       |
| Tier 5 | 13-15       | Mythic Shadow |

### Tier 1 - Basic Rogue

#### Identity

- New to stealth
- Quick but unrefined

#### Visual Style

- Light starter gear
- Small blades and utility pouches
- Minimal ornamentation

#### Tone

- Clever
- Cautious

#### Pose Direction

- Ready but compact stance
- Low center of gravity

#### Environment

- Starter alleys, training yards, or simple mission routes

### Tier 2 - Sneak

#### Identity

- Growing confidence
- Better route efficiency

#### Visual Style

- Cleaner cloak silhouette
- Better boots, belts, and hidden tools
- Slightly more dramatic stealth styling

#### Tone

- Quiet confidence
- Controlled mischief

#### Pose Direction

- Mid-step or low ready posture
- One tool or blade visible

#### Environment

- Markets, rooftops, or shadowed side routes

### Tier 3 - Assassin

#### Identity

- Efficient killer
- Clean execution specialist

#### Visual Style

- Sharper weapons
- Better leather layering
- Strong stealth silhouette

#### Tone

- Precise
- Dangerous

#### Pose Direction

- Action-ready
- Fast-strike framing

#### Environment

- Forest shadows, alleys, stealth missions, or moonlit paths

### Tier 4 - Phantom

#### Identity

- Hard to track
- High-control infiltrator

#### Visual Style

- Elegant stealth gear
- Low-reflection materials
- Refined magical or surreal accents

#### Tone

- Untouchable
- Cool under pressure

#### Pose Direction

- Dynamic, balanced, and confident
- Strong silhouette breakup

#### Environment

- Bazaar lights, mirror halls, or surreal stealth zones

### Tier 5 - Mythic Shadow

#### Identity

- Legendary infiltrator
- Efficiency made iconic

#### Visual Style

- Distinctive mythic cloak or veil
- Signature blades and accessories
- Cinematic shadow effects

#### Tone

- Elusive
- Larger-than-life

#### Pose Direction

- Central but low-profile heroic framing
- Motion implied even at rest

#### Environment

- High-end mythic locations
- Shadow-heavy or dreamlike final-scene compositions

## Gear Integration

Character visuals dynamically update based on:

- Equipped weapon
- Equipped armor
- Key artifacts

### Dynamic Visual Update Rules

- Weapons should feel compact, stylish, and visible without destroying stealth silhouette
- Armor should emphasize motion over bulk
- Accessories should add subtle utility cues, coin hints, smoke tools, or charm effects
